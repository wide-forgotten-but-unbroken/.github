<h1>Forgotten but Unbroken - Overview</h1>

<h2>Purpose of the game</h2>
<p><strong>Forgotten but Unbroken</strong> is a Windows turn‑based tactical strategy game developed by Centurion Developments and published by MicroProse Software. It immerses players in the role of a resistance commander during World War II, leading a small squad of fighters against Axis occupation forces, while managing base operations, resources, morale, and the fates of your soldiers.</p>

<h2>Main Features</h2>
<ul>
  <li><strong>Turn‑based tactical combat</strong> - The game delivers realistic, strategic combat where positioning, cover, unit abilities, and resource management matter. Enemy units (including elite SS troops) pose serious, morale‑affecting threats, making each decision weighty.</li>
  <li><strong>Squad and soldier management</strong> - Players command a customizable squad comprised of six soldier classes. Each soldier has unique traits, perks and vulnerabilities (e.g. morale, health, susceptibility to weather or injury), affecting performance in missions.</li>
  <li><strong>Base management</strong> - Between missions, you manage a hidden resistance base: build facilities (hospitals, armories), heal wounded soldiers, maintain morale, manage supplies, and survive weather or resource shortages.</li>
  <li><strong>Historical narrative and real‑world context</strong> - The game draws on real WWII events and stories of unsung heroes. Periodic "newspaper" reports and narrative events like battles or historical operations intersect with your campaign, grounding your choices in a believable historical context.</li>
  <li><strong>Diplomacy, alliances and strategic decisions</strong> - Through successful missions and diplomacy, you may gain support from allies, who can send elite units to assist you in difficult battles - adding depth and strategic variability to long-term planning.</li>
  <li><strong>Dynamic campaign & replayability</strong> - With base management, soldier traits, evolving morale, varied mission types (defense, stealth, ambush, sabotage), and no magical healing, each play‑through unfolds differently depending on your choices and how you manage your squad.</li>
</ul>
